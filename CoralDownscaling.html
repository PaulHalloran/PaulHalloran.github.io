Downscaling climate projections to coral reefs


Coral reef managers have become skilled users of tools like Coral Reef Watch's 5km resolution satellite-based coral heat stress products, yet planning for the impacts of climate change on coral reefs relies on the output of global climate models with resolutions of ~100km and which contain no representation of coastal processes. Statistical tools have been used to add detail to projections, but they are doing so with no additional information about what is actually going on the coastal shelf, and simply assuming that the present day spatial patterns will persist into the future. Traditional cutting edge coastal models are being used to understand processes and producing short-range projections, but they are too computationally expensive to run over the timescales we need, across the range of possible futures we may experience and across the spatial-scales needed to provide global-level joined-up guidance.

By stripping coastal models down to the essentials, and building back in those processes which are of 1st order importance we are developing a coastal model which can be run globally, can be run for decades or hundreds of years into the future, and can be run dozens or hundreds of times to explore scenario or forcing uncertainty.
